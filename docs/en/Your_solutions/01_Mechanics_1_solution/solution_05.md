This problem is all about **Vector Addition**. To go "directly North," the boat must fight the current. If the boat points straight North, the river will sweep it East. Therefore, the boat must aim slightly **West** to cancel out the river's motion.

---

### 1. Determining the Direction (Angle)

Think of this as a right-angled triangle.

* The **Hypotenuse** is the boat's speed in still water ($5 \text{ m/s}$).
* The **Opposite side** (the part fighting the river) must be $2 \text{ m/s}$ to the West.
* The **Adjacent side** is the resulting speed toward the North.

To find the angle $\theta$ (West of North):


$$\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{v_{\text{river}}}{v_{\text{boat}}} = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$

Using a calculator for the inverse sine:


$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx \mathbf{23.58^\circ}$$

The boat should head at an angle of **$23.58^\circ$ West of North.**

---

### 2. Determining the Time to Cross

To find the time, we need to know the **ground speed** (how fast it is actually moving North). We use the Pythagorean theorem:


$$v_{\text{North}}^2 + v_{\text{river}}^2 = v_{\text{boat}}^2$$

$$v_{\text{North}}^2 + 2^2 = 5^2$$

$$v_{\text{North}}^2 = 25 - 4 = 21$$

$$v_{\text{North}} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}$$

Now, use the basic formula $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$:


$$t = \frac{200 \text{ m}}{4.58 \text{ m/s}} \approx \mathbf{43.64 \text{ s}}$$

---

### Explanation of Symbols

* $v_{\text{river}}$ ($2 \text{ m/s}$): The velocity of the water relative to the shore.
* $v_{\text{boat}}$ ($5 \text{ m/s}$): The "engine speed" of the boat relative to the water.
* $v_{\text{North}}$: The "resultant velocity"—the actual speed the boat moves across the river as seen by someone on the shore.
* $\theta$: The heading angle needed to compensate for the drift.
