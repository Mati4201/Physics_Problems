
## Question 5: Relative Velocity

**The Setup**

* River velocity ($v_r$): **2 m/s** (East)
* Boat velocity ($v_b$): **5 m/s** (in still water)
* River width ($d$): **200 m**

**What do these symbols mean?**

* **Relative Velocity:** The boat's actual movement over the ground is the combination (vector sum) of its own engine speed and the river pushing it.
* **Heading Angle ($\theta$):** The angle the boat must steer *into* the current so it doesn't get swept downstream.

**1. Heading Direction**
To travel straight North, the boat must fight the eastward current. Its westward speed must perfectly cancel the river's eastward speed ($2 \text{ m/s}$).


$$v_b \sin(\theta) = v_r \implies 5 \sin(\theta) = 2$$

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$

$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ$$


The boat must head **23.58° West of North**.

**2. Crossing Time**
Since part of the boat's **5 m/s** engine power is used to fight the current, its actual speed moving straight North ($v_{North}$) is lower. We use the Pythagorean theorem:


$$v_{North} = \sqrt{v_b^2 - v_r^2} = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{25 - 4} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}$$

Now, use basic distance and speed to find the time:


$$t = \frac{d}{v_{North}} = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}} \approx 43.64 \text{ s}$$
