

## Question 8: Circular Motion

**The Setup**

* Radius of Earth ($R$): **6,378 km = 6,378,000 m**
* Time for one full rotation ($T$): **24 hours = 86,400 s**

**What do these symbols mean?**

* **Angular Velocity ($\omega$):** How fast the Earth is spinning in angles per second (radians per second).
* **Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$):** The "center-seeking" acceleration required to keep an object moving in a circle. Without gravity providing this inward pull, a person on the equator would fly off into space in a straight line!

**1. Angular Velocity**
First, we find how many radians the Earth spins through per second. One full circle is $2\pi$ radians.


$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$$

**2. Centripetal Acceleration**
Now, plug the angular velocity and the radius into the centripetal acceleration formula:


$$a_c = \omega^2 R$$

$$a_c = (7.272 \times 10^{-5})^2 \cdot 6378000 \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**Conclusion:** A person standing on the equator experiences an inward acceleration of **0.0337 m/s²** just to stay moving in a circle with the Earth's surface. (Since gravity pulls down at $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$, this tiny $0.0337$ deduction is why you actually weigh slightly less at the equator than at the poles!).
