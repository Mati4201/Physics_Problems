


### 8. Circular Motion

**The Setup:**
We need to find the centripetal acceleration ($a_c$) of a person standing exactly on the Earth's equator. As the Earth spins, this person travels in a giant circle. 

To solve this, we need two key pieces of information: the radius of that circle and the time it takes to complete one full revolution.

---

**Step 1: Identify and Convert the Known Variables**
In physics, it's always best to work in standard SI units (meters, kilograms, seconds) to ensure our final answer is in the correct units ($\text{m/s}^2$).

* **Radius ($R$):** The problem gives us the Earth's radius as **6378 km**. 
    Converting to meters: $R = 6378 \times 1000 = \textbf{6,378,000 m}$
* **Period ($T$):** The time it takes for the Earth to complete one full rotation is **24 hours**.
    Converting to seconds: $T = 24 \text{ hr} \times 60 \text{ min/hr} \times 60 \text{ s/min} = \textbf{86,400 s}$

---

**Step 2: Calculate the Angular Velocity ($\omega$)**
Angular velocity measures how fast an object is sweeping through angles (in radians) over time. One full circle is $2\pi$ radians.

$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}$$
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{86400}$$
$$\omega \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$$

---

**Step 3: Calculate Centripetal Acceleration ($a_c$)**
Centripetal acceleration is the acceleration directed toward the center of the circular path that keeps the object moving in a circle. The formula using angular velocity is:

$$a_c = R\omega^2$$

Now, plug in our values:
$$a_c = 6378000 \times (7.272 \times 10^{-5})^2$$
$$a_c \approx 6378000 \times (5.288 \times 10^{-9})$$
$$a_c \approx \textbf{0.0337 m/s}^2$$

**Fun context:** This acceleration is directed toward the center of the Earth. It's what slightly reduces the normal force you feel from the ground at the equator compared to the poles! However, because it's so incredibly small compared to the acceleration due to gravity ($g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$), we barely notice it and definitely don't fly off into space.
