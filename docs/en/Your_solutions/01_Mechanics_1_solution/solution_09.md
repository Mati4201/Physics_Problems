### 9. Momentum Comparison

**The Setup:**
We need to compare the momentum of two very different objects: a fast, tiny fly and a slow, heavier tennis ball. 

The formula for momentum ($p$) is simply the product of an object's mass ($m$) and its velocity ($v$):
$$p = mv$$

To make a proper comparison, it's a good habit to convert all masses to the standard SI unit of kilograms (kg).

---

**Step 1: Calculate the Fly's Momentum**
* **Mass ($m$):** 2 grams = **0.002 kg**
* **Velocity ($v$):** **10 m/s**

$$p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002 \times 10$$
$$p_{\text{fly}} = \textbf{0.02 kg}\cdot\textbf{m/s}$$

---

**Step 2: Calculate the Tennis Ball's Momentum**
* **Mass ($m$):** 60 grams = **0.060 kg**
* **Velocity ($v$):** **1 m/s**

$$p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \times 1$$
$$p_{\text{ball}} = \textbf{0.06 kg}\cdot\textbf{m/s}$$

---

**Step 3: Compare**
Even though the fly is moving ten times faster than the tennis ball, the tennis ball is thirty times more massive. 

Comparing the two results ($0.06 > 0.02$), we can clearly see that the **tennis ball** has the greater momentum. It would take more force to stop the slow-moving tennis ball than the fast-moving fly!
