

## Question 9: Momentum Comparison

**The Setup**

* **The Fly:** Mass = **2 g**, Velocity = **10 m/s**
* **The Tennis Ball:** Mass = **60 g**, Velocity = **1 m/s**

**What do these symbols mean?**

* **Momentum ($p$):** The quantity of motion an object has. Think of it as how hard it would be to stop the object.
* The formula is **$p = m \cdot v$** (mass times velocity).
* *Crucial Step:* In physics, mass must always be calculated in **kilograms (kg)**, not grams!

**1. The Fly's Momentum**
Convert **2 g** to kg by dividing by 1000: $m = 0.002$ kg.


$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \cdot 10 = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

**2. The Tennis Ball's Momentum**
Convert **60 g** to kg by dividing by 1000: $m = 0.06$ kg.


$$p_{ball} = 0.06 \cdot 1 = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

**Conclusion:** The **tennis ball** has three times more momentum than the fly! Even though the fly is moving ten times faster, the tennis ball is thirty times heavier, which easily wins the momentum battle.
