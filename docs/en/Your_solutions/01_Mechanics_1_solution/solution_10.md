In physics, **Momentum ($p$)** is the measure of how difficult it is to stop a moving object. It depends on two things: how heavy it is (**mass**) and how fast it is going (**velocity**).

---

## The Calculation

To compare the two fairly, we must first convert all units to the standard metric system (kilograms and meters).

### 1. The Fly

* **Mass ($m$):** $2\text{ grams} = 0.002\text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v$):** $10\text{ m/s}$
* **Momentum formula:** $p = m \times v$

$$p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002 \times 10 = \mathbf{0.02\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$



### 2. The Tennis Ball

* **Mass ($m$):** $60\text{ grams} = 0.06\text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v$):** $11\text{ m/s}$
* **Momentum formula:** $p = m \times v$

$$p_{\text{ball}} = 0.06 \times 1 = \mathbf{0.06\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$



---

## The Verdict

The **tennis ball** has **3 times more momentum** than the fly. Even though the fly is moving ten times faster, its mass is so incredibly small that it doesn't carry much "impact" compared to the heavier ball.

---

## Explanation of Letters

* **$p$**: Momentum (the letter $m$ was already taken for mass, so $p$ is used from the Latin word *petere*, meaning "to go towards").
* **$m$**: Mass (the amount of matter in the object).
* **$v$**: Velocity (speed with a specific direction).
* **$\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$**: The standard unit for momentum (kilogram-meters per second).
