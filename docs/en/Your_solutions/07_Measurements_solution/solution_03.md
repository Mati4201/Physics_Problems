

### 1. Calculate the Absolute Uncertainty

The uncertainty is 5% of the speedometer reading (60 km/h).

$$ \Delta v = 60 \times \left(\frac{5}{100}\right) $$

$$ \Delta v = 3 \text{ km/h} $$

This means the speedometer's reading could be off by exactly 3 km/h in either direction.

### 2. Determine the Range

To find the actual speed range, subtract and add the absolute uncertainty to the original reading:

* **Minimum possible speed:** $60 - 3 = 57 \text{ km/h}$
* **Maximum possible speed:** $60 + 3 = 63 \text{ km/h}$

The range of the car's actual speed is **57 km/h to 63 km/h**.
