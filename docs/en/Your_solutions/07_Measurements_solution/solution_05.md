

### 1. Identify the Values

* **Measured value ($t$):** $5.45$ seconds
* **Absolute uncertainty ($\Delta t$):** $0.22$ seconds

### 2. Calculate the Percentage Uncertainty

The formula for percentage uncertainty is:


$$ \text{Percentage Uncertainty} = \left( \frac{\Delta t}{t} \right) \times 100\% $$

Substituting the given values into the formula:


$$ \text{Percentage Uncertainty} = \left( \frac{0.22}{5.45} \right) \times 100\% $$

$$ \text{Percentage Uncertainty} \approx 0.04036 \times 100\% $$

$$ \text{Percentage Uncertainty} \approx 4.04\% $$

### 3. Final Result

Standard practice is to round percentage uncertainties to one or two significant figures (matching the given absolute uncertainty).

The percentage uncertainty of the time measurement is **$4.0\%$**.
